# Procedure Templates Repository

This folder contains comprehensive patient information documents for common otolaryngology procedures. These documents serve as a reference repository that can be used for any patient undergoing these procedures.

## Purpose

- **Patient Education**: Provide detailed information about surgical procedures
- **Informed Consent**: Support the informed consent process
- **Reference Material**: Quick access to procedure details, risks, and post-operative care
- **Standardization**: Ensure consistent information delivery across all patients

## Available Documents

### 1. Tonsillectomy
- **File**: `tonsillectomy.md`
- **Covers**: Indications, procedure details, post-operative care, risks/complications, recovery timeline
- **Use For**: Adult and pediatric tonsillectomy patients

### 2. Septoplasty
- **File**: `septoplasty.md`
- **Covers**: Indications, surgical technique, healing process, potential complications, success rates
- **Use For**: Patients with deviated nasal septum requiring correction

## How to Use

### For Healthcare Providers

1. **Pre-Operative Counseling**: Review relevant sections with patient before surgery
2. **Patient Handouts**: Print or email to patients for home review
3. **Informed Consent**: Reference when explaining procedure risks
4. **Post-Operative Instructions**: Use care sections as discharge instructions
5. **Answering Questions**: Quick reference for common patient concerns

### For Patients

- Read thoroughly before your procedure
- Review warning signs and emergency contact information
- Prepare questions for your surgeon
- Keep post-operative care instructions accessible during recovery
- Share with family members who will help with your care

## Document Format

All documents are written in Markdown (.md) format and include:

- **Comprehensive Overview**: What the procedure is and why it's done
- **Pre-Operative Preparation**: How to prepare for surgery
- **Procedure Details**: What happens during surgery
- **Post-Operative Care**: Day-by-day recovery instructions
- **Risk Stratification**: Common, uncommon, and rare complications
- **Warning Signs**: When to seek immediate medical attention
- **Expected Outcomes**: Success rates and recovery timelines
- **Special Considerations**: Age, medical conditions, other factors

## Converting to PDF

To convert these documents to PDF for printing or distribution:

```bash
# Using pandoc (recommended)
pandoc tonsillectomy.md -o tonsillectomy.pdf

# Or use online converters like:
# - https://www.markdowntopdf.com/
# - https://md2pdf.netlify.app/
```

## Adding New Procedures

When adding new procedure documents, follow this structure:

1. **Introduction**: Brief description of the procedure
2. **Indications**: When the procedure is recommended
3. **Pre-Operative Preparation**: Instructions before surgery
4. **The Procedure**: What happens during surgery
5. **Post-Operative Care**: Recovery instructions by timeframe
6. **Risks and Complications**: Organized by frequency (common/uncommon/rare)
7. **Warning Signs**: Emergency symptoms requiring immediate attention
8. **Expected Outcomes**: Success rates and patient satisfaction
9. **Special Considerations**: Populations requiring special attention
10. **Questions to Ask**: Prompts for patient-surgeon discussion
